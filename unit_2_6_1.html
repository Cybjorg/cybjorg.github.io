To begin this lesson let's go on a walkabout!  A walkabout is a term used to describe an informal stroll usually to tour a specific area.

In this case, we will go on a digital walkabout looking at a variety of images showing the the use of technology.  Some may strike you as positive, negative, or simply intriguing.  Regardless, the important part of this opening activity is to start thinking about how technology can and should be used in teaching and learning.

Teaching Technique

A visual walkabout can be a powerful teaching technique to start one of your units, as it allows students to demonstrate their current understanding on a topic, focus on a big idea or concept, make connections to their prior knowledge, and set a general tone of inquiry.

For more information on the visual walkabout teaching technique visit this article by Discovery Education.

Activity

Take a 'digital walkabout' and view all of the images below. Note that you can click on the image to see it full screen.  Share a question, comment, and/or connection for at least one of the images by clicking on the + icon. Include your name on your post.
