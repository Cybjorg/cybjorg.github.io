In this model, technology use is categorized into one of four categories from the lowest level (substitution) to the highest level (redefinition). Although not always possible, the goal is to reach the top levels of this model and use technology to transform education.

Although the SAMR model can be a valuable tool to encourage teachers and students to use technology to redefine tasks in an innovative manner, one criticism of this model is that it puts too much of the focus on the technology tool rather than the learning target and educational outcomes desired.

Remember, effective planning must always consider the key educational outcomes. In most cases, reaching these desired outcomes occurs best when students take an active approach to their learning by exploring, collaborating and creating. Fortunately, there are many technology tools to get students to do exactly these things.
