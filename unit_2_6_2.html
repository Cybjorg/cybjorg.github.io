Why use technology in the classroom?

One way to answer this question is based on the belief that schools and teachers need to help our students use the technology available to them in society to become productive citizens. In other words, effective teachers should create opportunities for students to learn important digital and technology literacy skills that is already part of their culture.

Think About It

Many national curriculums and international organizations have outlined a set of technology and digital skills and standards for students. Take the International Baccalaureate Organization (IBO) for an example. The IBO specifically references the following technology and digital literacy skills in their Approaches to Learning (ATL) Skills Framework within the document MYP (Middle Years Programme): From Principles to Practice.

  • Participate in, and contribute to, digital social media networks
  • Collaborate with peers and experts using a variety of digital environments and media
  • Share ideas with multiple audiences using a variety of digital environments and media
  • Use social media networks appropriately to build and develop relationships
  • Select and use technology effectively and productively
  • Evaluate and select information sources and digital tools based on their appropriateness to specific tasks
  • Understand and use technology systems
  • Locate, organize, analyse, evaluate, synthesize and ethically use information from a variety of sources and media (including digitalsocial media and online networks)
  • Demonstrate awareness of media interpretations of events and ideas (including digital social media)

Need another example?  Have a look at the International Society for Technology in Education standards for students.
